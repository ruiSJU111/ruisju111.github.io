Module 10: Applications and Ethics in Computer Vision

1. Learning Objectives

  • Explore impactful real-world applications of computer vision
  • Understand the societal implications and risks of vision-based systems
  • Evaluate ethical concerns in CV applications such as surveillance and facial recognition
  • Reflect on bias, fairness, and responsible AI development

2. Key Applications of Computer Vision

2.1 Optical Character Recognition (OCR)

  • Used for digitizing printed and handwritten documents
  • Applications: Scanned books, ID documents, bank cheques
  • Popular libraries: Tesseract, EasyOCR

2.2 Facial Recognition

  • Used in security, device unlocking, and user verification
  • Controversial use in law enforcement and mass surveillance
  • Bias against certain demographics is a major ethical concern

2.3 Medical Imaging

  • Assists in diagnosing diseases from X-rays, MRIs, CT scans
  • Early detection of cancer, tumors, and anomalies
  • Models must be explainable and carefully validated

2.4 Autonomous Vehicles

  • Uses cameras and LiDAR for lane detection, object tracking
  • Critical for decision-making in real-time environments
  • Failure can result in serious accidents or harm

3. Ethical Concerns in Computer Vision

3.1 Bias and Fairness

  • Training data often overrepresents certain demographics
  • Vision systems may fail on underrepresented groups
  • Example: Facial recognition having lower accuracy on darker-skinned faces

3.2 Privacy and Consent

  • Surveillance systems often operate without explicit user consent
  • Smart cameras track individuals in public/private spaces
  • Data protection and anonymity are essential considerations

3.3 Explainability

  • Deep learning systems are often black boxes
  • Important to provide interpretable results, especially in high-risk applications like healthcare

3.4 Deepfakes and Misinformation

  • GANs can create highly realistic fake videos and images
  • Misused to spread propaganda, fake news, or manipulate public opinion

4. Case Study: Amazon Rekognition

Amazon Rekognition is a facial analysis and recognition service.

  • Tested by law enforcement and governments
  • Criticized for racial bias and wrongful identifications
  • Led to bans and calls for regulation

Lesson: Commercial CV systems must be tested thoroughly and ethically before deployment.

5. Discussion: Responsible AI for Vision

Considerations for building ethical CV systems:

  • Diverse and representative training datasets
  • Regular auditing and fairness evaluations
  • User transparency and opt-in mechanisms
  • Regulations and policy frameworks for high-risk use cases
