PALM-GLOBAL: Cross-domain Palm Leaf Analysis

A Unified Framework for Analyzing Historical Scripts from Southeast Asia and India

Project Summary

The PALM-GLOBAL project represents a significant expansion of our research into palm leaf manuscript analysis. This initiative moves beyond a single region to establish a unified, cross-domain framework for analyzing historical scripts from diverse geographical and linguistic origins.

Our work now encompasses not only Southeast Asian scripts like Khmer, Balinese, and Sundanese, but also extends to South Indian scripts such as Tamil and Malayalam. By studying these varied manuscript traditions together, we aim to develop more robust and generalized models that can handle the unique challenges posed by different writing systems, materials, and degradation patterns. This cross-domain approach is crucial for building truly comprehensive tools for the global digital humanities community.
